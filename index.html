What is Carbon Dioxide?

A colorless gas

We cant talk about what our Carbon di oxide footprint is without first knowing what it is. In short, Carbon di oxide (chemical formula CO2) is a colorless gas with a density about 53% higher than that of dry air.

Its effect

Carbon dioxide molecules consist of a carbon atom covalently double bonded to two oxygen atoms. Carbon di oxide is responsible for 0,04% of our atmosphere, which to the plain eye might look really small, but as we”ll see, it has a much bigger effect than you may think

The molecule

Where can we find CO2 emissions?

We all think that CO2 comes only from our breathing and cars, but humans emit two tons of CO2 each year just by eating. But that’s not the only strange way we release emissions into the atmosphere. The Corona Virus pandemic has also changed Co2 emissions. U.K’s Institution of Engineering and Technology claims that working from home increases CO2 emissions for many workers. That’s because telecommuting can increase home energy use by up to 30%–and many workers who telecommute a few days of the week tend to move further away from the office, increasing urban sprawl and upping travel time when they do head in to see the boss The rise of Co2 from pre industrial ages The current concentration is about 0.04% (412 ppm) by volume, having risen from pre-industrial levels of 280 ppm. Natural sources include volcanoes, hot springs and geysers, and it is freed from carbonate rocks by dissolution in water and acids. Because carbon dioxide is soluble in water, it occurs naturally in groundwater, rivers and lakes, ice caps, glaciers and seawater. It is present in deposits of petroleum and natural gas.
